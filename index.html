A citizen movement for a quieter India

From Blow Horn to Om Shanti

India gave the world the word for peace — शांति. Yet every truck on our roads still orders us to Blow Horn. We're here to change what our roads say, and how they sound.

No donations. No politics. We will never ask you for money — only for your voice.

Our roads are loud, and it's costing us more than our calm.

Constant honking isn't just noise. It's a daily assault on our hearing, our nerves, and our cities — and it's so normal we've stopped noticing. We think it's time we noticed.

80–120 dB

A single truck horn. The safe limit for a residential street is 55 dB. Above 85 dB, the damage to your hearing is permanent. [cite: CPCB]

1.5 lakh+

Lives lost on Indian roads every year. Roads this dangerous shouldn't also be this hostile. [cite: MoRTH]

Every day

Millions of trucks roll past schools, hospitals and homes carrying two words painted on the back: Blow Horn.

Ask anyone who lives by a highway, any parent of a baby who won't sleep, anyone with a headache by 9 a.m. — they already know. We're just giving it a name.

We're not asking for silence. We're asking for three words to change.

For decades, India's commercial vehicles have carried signage that invites honking — "Blow Horn," "Horn OK Please," "Horn Please." It trains a whole nation to lean on the horn. Our ask is simple, specific, and needs no new law: replace that signage with a message that asks for the opposite.

Stop Horn
No Horn
Om Shantipeace, for the road

One change, on the back of every truck. From a command to honk — to a reminder to breathe.

This isn't a dream. It's already been done once.

In 2015, Maharashtra's Transport Commissioner ordered "Horn OK Please" removed from commercial vehicles across the state — with a single administrative circular. No new law. No years of debate. Just one decision, by one office that understood it mattered.

If one state can do it with the stroke of a pen, the country can too. We just have to ask — together, and loudly enough to be heard. (Quietly, of course.)
